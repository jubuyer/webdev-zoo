Toucan

A toucan is a type of bird that has 40 different species found in South and Central America as they thrive in rainforest canopies. Among them, the Toco Toucan is the most recognizable for its black body, white throat, and vibrant orange bill. They range in size from 7 inches to a little over 2 feet tall. They live in tree hollows and are not the best fliers so they spend a lot of their time hopping along in the trees. A toucan's bill (beak) is made from keratin - it helps them regulate blood flow to keep their bodies cooler and quite handy for eating, mating, self-defense, and more. Toucans are among the world's noisest birds and they use their bills to make tapping and clattering noises.

Fun fact: Toucan chicks are pale-colored and born blind.
