Puffin

Puffins are seabirds that primarily live and breed in Northern Alaska. They populate coastal cliffs or offshore islands, living in large colonies that can be found in deeper parts of the land. Puffins have special beaks made to catch fish, which they hunt by diving into the water. They can both fly and swim extremely fast, reaching speeds of up to 55 miles per hour in the air and 10 miles per hour in the water.

The beaks of puffins change throughout the year, ranging from dull grey in the winter to bright orange in the spring. The bright beak color helps attract mates, leading to lifelong pairings following a mating ritual of rubbing their beaks together.
